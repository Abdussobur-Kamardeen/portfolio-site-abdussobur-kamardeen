Swift Cooks ★ ★ ★

Overview

People have started to realise that they need to be better with their intakes of different foods. The idea of making home meals have been seen has overwhelming and time consuming.

Swift Cooks is a recipe web app that includes different meals and recipes for users looking to change their diet and find quick easy meals. They would have quick meals options that meets their requirements to make things swift and efficient for them.

Role

UX/UI DesignerUser Research, Competitive Analysis, Rapid Prototyping, Visual Design, User Testing, Wireframing, Interactive Design and Mockups

Details

Duration: 2 months, December-Feb 2023

Tools: Figma, Keynote

About the app ★ ★ ★

Swift Cooks is a recipe based app for consumers looking to change their diet or make quick meals, especially when they don`t have time to think about what to eat. If you dont know where to start, this app is here to make that process easier by offering simple steps for a beginner.

Problem

People have started to realise that they need to be better with their intakes of different foods. The idea of making home meals have been seen has overwhelming in this. Nowadays, people are cautious about their nutrients intake but they don`t where to start. Some people feel lazy to have to think about what to eat. So, they aren`t as bothered to cook.

Solution

An app that gives users the ability to choose any recipe of choice depending on their time and schedule. Providing them with easy timing filters for meals that are quick. There will also be features that help beginners who don`t know where to start.

Competitive Analysis ★ ★ ★

I ran a competitive analysis on Kitchen stories and My new roots. These are my following thoughts:

Strengths

  • Users are able to upload their own recipes and share them.
  • They take into consideration day time, diet, dish and more to give users a variety of options to choose from.
  • Sharing stories and videos.
  • They have community for users to engage with.

Weaknesses

  • The home page is jam-packed with a lot of content.
  • The more from option shouldn`t be so bold in the middle of the home page.

Strengths

  • Offers online classes.
  • Variety of recipe options including seasonal recipes.
  • Offers cookbooks.
  • You can work close to with the founder.

Weaknesses

  • The layout of the recipes is too clustered and made as a long list.
  • No sign up page.
  • No preview of dishes on recipes page.
  • Doesn`t look as interactive.

Approach ★ ★ ★

The approach I took was making the website seem more user friendly/engaging. By offering previews so users always know what they are looking for. Where, they could have different food categories previewed in the home page. It will be a bit more interactive to users. My web app will be set apart by making it less compact. It wouldn`t have too much content in the home page. Only the main things the users will be interested in. There`s a lot of vegan people now and knowing they have an option it makes them get to their destinations quicker. So, the app is meant to cater to a variety of people.

Feedback ★ ★ ★

Good

I really enjoy the overall layout and spacing of all elements in the design. The interface is clean and easy to follow, and your choice of typeface is exceptionally readable.

I really like the layout of your app, the header image fits really well with your text and colour scheme. I really like how you used the spoon icons to show the difficulty of the recipe. I like the way your logo expands as the page gets larger. Doesn`t distract from the content and is visually pleasing

Constructive

When reading the ingredients and steps, my eyes felt a bit exhausted trying to keep up with the text.

It looks like there are three different typefaces being used, it would be more consistent to limit your typefaces to one or two and use sizing/weights to show hierarchy.

Challenges ★ ★ ★

The challenges I faced were design challenges and knowing exactly what to include in the final screens to make sure all the user requirements were met. As I`m someone who hasn`t been ona recipe app before it was a bit challenging to know exactly how to display what consumers need. I am someone who has always wanted to look for things to cook, but I`m not the masses which meant I had to try and look at different perspectives that could somewhat align with the consumers. I overcame these when I conducted some user testing and interviews to ask my tutor, mentor and peers in the UX/UI community for help. Their feedback gave me a different insight on how to approach it using UX.

Takeaways ★ ★ ★

Creating this recipe web app has shown me various way to critically think like a user. It was a great experience that made me question myself. Do I make design decisions based on what I think the consumers want to see or what the consumer actually want to see? For a better experience. It`s easy to confuse the two because I thought about the ideas that consumers could want without actually thinking about the consumers were seeking from other recipe apps visually that my app could provide. This made it a positive experience to always think about the experience of consumers both critically and visually. So, that the apps could receive positive feedback.
